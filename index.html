Frequently Asked Questions

As a patient, you have the right to informed consent, which means you should be fully informed about your treatment options, risks, and benefits before agreeing to any procedure. You also have the right to privacy, meaning your medical records and personal information are protected by laws like HIPAA. Additionally, you have the right to seek a second opinion, refuse treatment, and file complaints if you believe your rights have been violated.

If you suspect you have been a victim of medical malpractice, it is important to act quickly. Document everything related to your treatment, including communications with healthcare providers and any adverse effects you’ve experienced. Then, consult with a legal professional who specializes in medical malpractice to review your case and determine your next steps.

Start by carefully reviewing your medical bill and comparing it to your insurance coverage. If you find discrepancies or charges you don’t understand, contact the billing department of the healthcare provider to request an itemized bill. If the issue isn’t resolved, you can appeal the charges with your insurance company or seek assistance from a patient advocate or legal professional.
